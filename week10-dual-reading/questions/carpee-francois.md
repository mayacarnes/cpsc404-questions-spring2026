Corporate hierarchy relies on authority to make decisions and it suggests that you should avoid going over your boss' head as much as possible and that you should have their permission. Open Source on the other hand relies on reputation and transparent discussion, anyone can raise concerns. In an open source project, does the person with the strongest reputation have the biggest influence? A benefit from open source projects is that they are inherently more democratic but since it lacks a formal hierarchy, it means that there is really no one you can go over, so where do you draw the boundaries if there are? So that you avoid going over people's heads. I believe that contribution and respect would play an important role but sometimes that might not be enough.
