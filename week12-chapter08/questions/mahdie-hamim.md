# Week 12 Question - Chapter 8: Your Boss Is Not Your Friend

**Author:** Hamim Mahdie  

## Question

Chapter 8 warns against treating your boss like a friend—especially highlighting the risks of oversharing, blurred boundaries, and how quickly dynamics shift when a peer becomes your manager. But in my experience, especially during internships, the most effective managers often *intentionally* create a friendly, informal environment to build trust and openness.

This creates a tension: if a manager encourages personal sharing and a “we’re all friends” culture, how is an employee supposed to know where the real boundary is—especially when that same information (e.g., frustrations, doubts, or personal struggles) can later influence evaluations or trust?

In other words, is the responsibility entirely on the employee to maintain emotional distance, or does Chapter 8 underestimate how much managers actively shape—and sometimes exploit—this ambiguity? And if a friendship genuinely exists before a promotion, is it ever actually possible to “reset” into a professional relationship without one side being disadvantaged?
